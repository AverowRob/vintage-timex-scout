How a score is decided — the rule brick

Your Scout scores each listing 0–100 for taste fit — a holistic AI judgment, not a fixed formula. Price, brand and condition are handled by the gate, so the score is purely about taste fit. The bands:

90–100Standoutstrong alignment with your brief — a clear collab / character / advertising dial, deadstock / NOS, or a rare, distinctive model. These are your Top contenders.
70–89On-tastea named vintage model line in good shape.
40–69Ordinarya vintage Timex, nothing special.
0–39Off-tastegeneric / modern, digital, straps, watch lots — and anything you’ve passed on. A passed-on trait overrides taste: pass on “Mickey Mouse” and every Mickey watch lands here, even though character dials are normally on-taste.

Signals it weighs (from your brief): model lines (Marlin, Viscount, Camper…), character / advertising / novelty dials, collabs, deadstock / NOS / boxed, distinctive dials (bullseye, linen), and condition — plus your liked & passed-on references. Each listing’s reason names the drivers — e.g. “deadstock NOS, boxed, advertising dial.”
